English Language Test (IELTS)

IELTS is a widely recognized English language proficiency test used by individuals who aim to study, work, or migrate to countries where English is the primary language of communication. It is jointly managed by the British Council, IDP: IELTS Australia, and Cambridge Assessment English. The test measures the ability to understand, speak, read, and write in English through four sections: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. IELTS is accepted by more than 10,000 organizations worldwide, including universities, colleges, employers, immigration authorities, and professional bodies. It is recognized as a reliable and standard measure of English language proficiency.

▸ Know More
  • IELTS offers two modules: Academic and General Training. The Academic module is suitable for individuals applying to study at the undergraduate or postgraduate level. The General Training module is designed for those seeking work experience, training programs, or migration to English-speaking countries.
  • IELTS employs rigorous testing procedures and professional examiners, ensuring accurate assessment of language skills. The test evaluates various aspects of English proficiency, providing a comprehensive evaluation of a candidate's abilities.
  • IELTS scores are valid for two years from the test date. The test's versatility allows individuals to use their results for multiple purposes, such as university admissions, visa applications, professional registration, and employment opportunities.

IELTS Test Format

The four sections of IELTS assess different language skills:

  • Listening: Measures the ability to understand spoken English through a range of audio recordings.
  • Reading: Evaluates comprehension skills by presenting passages from different sources, such as books, newspapers, and magazines.
  • Writing: Requires test-takers to complete writing tasks, including essays or letters, to demonstrate their ability to express ideas clearly and coherently.

IELTS Band

IELTS scores are reported on a nine-band scale, ranging from 1 (non-user) to 9 (expert user). Each band corresponds to a specific level of English proficiency, providing a clear indication of a candidate's language abilities.

Band Description
1 Non-User: Essentially no ability to use the language.
2 Intermittent User: Basic competence is limited to familiar situations.
3 Extremely Limited User: Conveys and understands only general meaning in very familiar situations.
4 Limited User: Basic competence is limited to familiar situations.
5 Modest User: Partial command of the language, coping with overall meaning in most situations.
6 Competent User: Effective command of the language, with occasional inaccuracies and misunderstandings.
7 Good User: Operational command of the language, with occasional inaccuracies and misunderstandings.
8 Very Good User: Fully operational command of the language with only occasional unsystematic inaccuracies and misunderstandings.
9 Expert User: Fully operational command of the language with no inaccuracies, misunderstandings, or inappropriacies.
